Back stateside! I'll write about my hijinks with Scots in Boston when I finish my trip, but after a few days back to get over my jet lag, my buddy and I went up for an annual music festival in Greenfield, Massachusetts, the [Green River Festival](https://greenriverfestival.com/). I'd never been before, but I saw Geese was headling, so of course I had to go. Actually, I found out about the festival through Spotify's "On Tour" feature, where it advertised that Geese were playing in Massachusetts on the 21st. So, I hit up my buddy, and he got us tickets to the festival. Spotify's feature advertised Geese as playing at noon, which gave us some grief down the road, but I'll cover that later.

So, we left the Greater Boston area around 7:30 AM, and began our drive to Western Mass. It was a pretty drive, quiet on a Sunday morning, and basically a straight shot down Route 2, so we put on my Geese CDs (well, *Getting Killed* and *Heavy Metal*), to get in the mood for the show. We decided to stop for breakfast at a motel/restaurant in Erving, Mass, [French King Restaurant and Motel](https://maps.app.goo.gl/xHgmDmwxL7FZwn216), which was a really nice spot. It had a very New England vibe to it, which made me feel quite a bit of nostalgia, staff were super friendly, and the food was great. I got the Eggs Benedict with hash and home fries, with some fresh strawberries on the side, all of which were amazing. Being in Massachusetts where it's actually good, I also got some cranberry juice, to which my buddy quipped "what are you on your period?" Sure am, buddy.

After that, we drove to the festival. There was a parking garage in town where we could park for free, and take a shuttle from there to the fairgrounds. The shuttle was just a classic American big yellow school bus, smell and green leather seats and all. That brought me back a bit. Once we got to the fairgrounds, we had to wait another hour before gates opened and they let us in. However, Geese were soundchecking as we were waiting for gates, so that was a nice little treat. They soundchecked *100 Horses*, *Gravity Blues*, *2122*, and *Jesse*. After finally being let in, we were met with a surprise though. Spotify had told us Geese were on at noon. However, that was just the festival start time. Geese were playing at 7:30. So what, we'll enjoy the festival and wait? Problem is, my buddy had a Father's Day dinner to get to back in Greater Boston, with a reservation for 6, so he couldn't be there. Since my buddy is the one who bought the tickets, only he got the email with the schedule, and he isn't as litigious about reading every email as I am. So that sucked. The kicker is, it was originally a Father's Day lunch, but he rescheduled it to be able to see Geese. Should have just kept it as a lunch. 

So, after kicking around the fairgrounds for a bit and buying some swag, checking out the markets, food trucks, we decided to head down to [Treehouse Brewing Company - Deerfield](https://maps.app.goo.gl/CTZ53LK2ryYmQ44BA) and at least get some good beers out of the trip. And good beers we got. I had an *Emperor Julius* and *The Greenest Green*, while my buddy got a *Sticks*. Everything was great, but that's no surprise there, we went to Treehouse for beers. After that, we were hungry and didn't feel like paying festival food truck fees, so we then went to [Holiday Pizza](https://maps.app.goo.gl/SSeCqzv73gFrDuPu5) for a bite to eat. I got a pepperoni pizza and my buddy got a sub, and we enjoyed everything there. After that, we went to CVS so I could print out a totem for the concert and my buddy could print out some documents for his motorcycle license. But they wouldn't print my totem because it had a "copyrighted character" on it. Oh well.

I checked the Uber app, and it was $88 for a taxi from Greenfield to Greater Boston, which wasn't completely terrible, so I said bye to my buddy as he dropped me off back at the fairgrounds, and we parted ways. I got to the main stage for the second half of the *Lucius* set, moving up closer to the stage wherever I saw a gap in the crowd. When the *Lucius* crowd cleared a bit, I moved up quite a bit more for the *Wednesday* set, who I had never heard before, but loved. Definitely recommend listening to them if you haven't. The crowd was very lively for *Wednesday*, and I got caught up in several mosh pits, which I tried to stay to the edge of and deflect people leaping at me, and the smaller people behind me using me as cover. I hadn't exactly been to many concerts going up, and of the concerts I'd been to, *Radiohead* and *Parni Valjak* aren't exactly bands people mosh to, so that was my first experience with that. Lovely. After *Wednesday* finished up their set, I moved up much closer to the stage, probably about ten people behind barricade, to get ready for *Geese*. However, the thing is, I had left my water bottle in my buddy's car, had been standing in the sun and dust for hours, and was quite dehydrated. When the crew came up on stage with a huge box of water bottles to put by the mics for the band, we all begged for water in the crowd, and after some relenting, they gave one of the two dozen water bottles to the crowd to be passed around. Of course, never made it anywhere near me. Eventually, I asked some of the guys near me if they could shout for some water, since my voice was already too weak to and I would need to leave the crowd if I didn't get water soon, and even if I could navigate out of a crowd that thick, I certainly wouldn't be able to get my spot back so close to the front. Thankfully, they obliged, one of them had a spare water bottle who he let me finish, legend, and another guy up front passed his water bottle back. Heroes. *Geese* came out shortly after that, and my throat was ready for *Mysterious Love* and *Trinidad.* *Geese*'s setlist was as follows:

The *Geese* set was amazing, and I was able to record much of it. You can find a playlist of some of my recordings [on Youtube here](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wNrrR-EWYTU&list=PLkN8kMy5BemAgdsmOY-Zj1bah0GMoeLf2). During *Getting Killed*, a huge mosh pit opened up in front of me, which I was able to use to advance to about three people behind barricade, where I remained for most of the show, which was awesome. You can see the difference in my position between the *Husbands* video and *Islands of Men*. The show was great, the band had great interaction with the crowd, us joking with them and them with us. Some of the more memorable moments were the crowd starting a "fuck ice" chant to which Cameron commented "people have been screaming way dumber things at us the whole tour, I thought you were saying 'ice fuck,' that's the capacity I'm running at. I can get behind both word orders." to which we started an "ice fuck" chant. (You can find the tail end of this interaction at the beginning of my [I See Myself](https://youtu.be/ShilZlYt6qo?si=8WDGTyiYDnHcUG9Y) recording.) They also played with some of the songs a bit, such as extending Trinidad with an extended rant about kidney stones, which we were all cracking up at. Overall, great show, was so excited to see *Geese*, looking forwards to seeing them again in Köln in August.

After the show ended, I booked it to the beverage tent, who were already packing up. I asked if they had any water left, and they had exactly one bottle, which they gave me for free. After thanking my saviors, I chugged that, and went to the water refill station to fill it up a few more times. Two other guys were there, and they commented that I seemed to have faired pretty well, as they were both in the mosh pit and covered with dirt and dust. I mentioned that I was basically at barricade, and while I had been spared the craziness of the mosh pit, every time the mosh pit formed the crowd trash compacted me into everyone else, and my back had completely given out.

After a lot more water, I decided to try to order an Uber. Prices had doubled since I looked earlier, but five cars were available. However, none of them seemed to want to take a drive to Greater Boston, even for $160 + a potential tip. After about an hour of searching and my phone getting to 3% battery, I decided to take a shuttle back to Greenfield center, and try one more time from there before finding a hotel to crash at. While I was searching though, a guy drove up to me and asked if I was looking for an Uber. I said that I was, and he told me if I paid him the fare directly, he'd be willing to take me back. I was a bit apprehensive at first, but he showed me his credentials, and I got in. The drive passed largely uneventfully, he got me back to my parents', and I immediately crashed.

Turns out I got acute laryngitis from the dust bowl that was Green River Festival main stage and overextending my vocal chords, which led to a fun week, but I think this was the most fun I ever had at a concert, so it was 100% worth it. Just wish my buddy didn't have to leave. *Wednesday* was an awesome find, *Geese* was even more fun than I could have expected, so thank you Green River, and my driving, drinking, and father-loving buddy, for the amazing day.
